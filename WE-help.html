THE 10 DO’S AND DONT’S FOR A SUCCESFULL PLACEMENT(ARTICLE)

An internship is usually a very important part of a student’s life. It gives them an in depth view of their future career and can often make or break the future employees in any profession. That’s why there are a couple of Do’s and a couple of Dont’s for a successful work placement. In this article we will be giving you 10 tips on what to do and what not to do in your work placement.

Do's

  1. An internship is a very important job as you are trusted with many tasks, and you have been allowed to be at that organization for as long as you need, while being taught and tutored. That’s why you should always be appreciative of the placement and to your employers. Thank them by sending them e-mails or giving them notes, but don’t do this too often, as it might seem overbearing
  2. Act Professional- Remember this is a professional setting and you must treat your fellow employees and managers with respect. It is expected that you behave professionally throughout your entire work experience, except for celebrations of some kinds, or at times when it seems appropriate.
  3. Be Communicative- You should always ask supervisors or fellow employees regarding whether they have a task that they want you to complete. Your time at the company is valuable, and you should try to get the most out of it.
  4. wear the proper clothing, this of course goes without saying, you can’t walk into an office wearing baggy sweatpants with a stained shirt. This shows your employer that you do not care about this opportunity that they have given you and that you are not fully motivated. This can reflect badly on you for future careers as a bad review from your employer might deter other businesses from hiring you.
  5. Your main goal during your internship is to pick up as much knowledge and experience as possible, this is why you should make sure you should learn during your time there, you can do this through a number of ways, such as memorization, taking notes, taking pictures and asking questions.

Dont’s

  1. Staying quiet: You shouldn’t be afraid to ask questions about the work you are doing if you are feeling lost or confused, you should be opened to learning new things, and not shy away from talking to new and other people.
  2. Slaking Off: Take every moment to stand out and stand above the rest, this elevates you to a higher standing in the eyes of the Supervisor, and you get better reviews for future careers, because of this if there is a job opening you may be one of the first people that comes to mind.
  3. Giving Up: If you give up on any project it shows your supervisor that you are not hardworking and are not motivated. This actively harms you, you should use all of your possible options, and when those are done look for new ones. However, don’t be afraid to ask for help every now and then, there is no shame in it, sometimes we all get stuck.
  4. Stay in touch with coworkers to continue hearing about projects, industry developments or for leads on jobs. Talk to your friends back on campus, too. Explaining your internship to others may reinforce the lessons you take away from the experience and give you a greater appreciation of all you accomplished. By following these dos while avoiding the don’ts, you’ll possess the confidence to walk the walk and talk the talk of an experienced professional. You’ll complete your internship knowing how to feel comfortable in the workplace, how to effectively communicate in the office and how to leave your coworkers and supervisors with a positive impression of you as a professional.
  5. No one is perfect. If a mistake is made, whether it is missing a deadline or omitting a person from an email blast, take ownership of the error. Offer solutions as to how you will rectify the issue and prevent others in the future.
